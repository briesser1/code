
# Selecting & Implementing a New Data Management System  
## For a Social Services Agency  
### With Supporting Change Management Literature

# Part I — Strategic & Operational Questions to Ask

Replacing a legacy system in a social services agency is rarely a technical problem. It is a **strategy, governance, workflow, and change management challenge**.

Below is a structured framework of considerations and questions to guide evaluation and implementation.

---

## 1️⃣ Start With Strategy, Not Software

Before evaluating vendors, clarify:

### Mission & Outcomes
- What problems are we trying to solve?
- What is broken in the current system?
  - Reporting?
  - Case management?
  - Compliance?
  - Usability?
- What are the measurable success outcomes 12–24 months post-implementation?
- Is the priority:
  - Operational efficiency
  - Regulatory compliance
  - Data analytics capability
  - Client experience
  - Funding reporting
  - All of the above?

> If these answers are unclear, pause vendor selection. Technology cannot compensate for unclear strategy.

---

## 2️⃣ Stakeholder Mapping

In social services agencies, different groups often have competing priorities:

| Stakeholder | Primary Concern |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Frontline case workers | Ease of use & documentation speed |
| Supervisors | Oversight & workflow control |
| Compliance/legal | Audit trails & reporting accuracy |
| Leadership | KPIs & outcomes |
| IT | Security & maintainability |
| Finance | Billing & funding alignment |

Ask:
- Who uses the system daily?
- Who owns the data?
- Who defines KPIs?
- Who signs regulatory submissions?
- Who is accountable if implementation fails?

---

## 3️⃣ Functional Requirements

### Case Management
- Does it support longitudinal client records?
- Multiple program enrollments?
- Service tracking & outcome measurement?
- Workflow rules and approvals?

### Documentation & Compliance
- Required state/federal reporting formats?
- Audit trails?
- Time-stamped edits?
- Role-based permissions?

### Reporting & Analytics
- Direct database access?
- API access?
- Export options (CSV/JSON)?
- Can it integrate into a data warehouse?
- Are dashboards configurable or proprietary?

---

## 4️⃣ Technical Architecture

- Cloud-based? (AWS/Azure/GCP?)
- Multi-tenant or single-tenant?
- REST APIs available?
- Data model structure?
- Backup policies?
- Disaster recovery (RTO/RPO)?
- Vendor roadmap transparency?

---

## 5️⃣ Data Migration (High-Risk Area)

- Who cleans legacy data?
- What historical depth migrates?
- Are attachments and notes included?
- Is there a rollback plan?
- Is there a data dictionary?
- Are client IDs consistent?

> Many implementations fail due to underestimated data migration complexity.

---

## 6️⃣ Integration Requirements

- State systems
- Medicaid billing
- Court systems
- Referral platforms
- HR/payroll
- External reporting systems

Ask:
- Real-time or batch integrations?
- API documentation?
- Webhooks?
- SFTP support?

---

## 7️⃣ Security & Compliance

Depending on agency type:
- HIPAA
- CJIS
- FERPA
- State child welfare rules

Ask:
- SOC 2 certification?
- Encryption at rest/in transit?
- MFA?
- Role-based access?
- Field-level masking?
- Data export rights?

---

## 8️⃣ Vendor Evaluation Questions

- How many similar agencies do you serve?
- What implementations struggled — and why?
- Who owns the data?
- What happens at contract termination?
- What is the long-term pricing model?
- Are customizations configuration-based or code-based?

---

## 9️⃣ Financial Considerations

Beyond license cost:

- Implementation fees
- Data migration fees
- Training costs
- Ongoing support
- Storage growth costs
- Integration costs
- Per-user pricing growth risk

---

## 🔟 Change Management (Critical Success Factor)

- Who is the executive sponsor?
- Is there a formal change management plan?
- Will training be mandatory?
- Are super-users identified?
- What is the internal communication plan?
- How will resistance be handled?

# Part III — Practical Application to a Social Services System Implementation

If you were to anchor your implementation in research:

### Use Lewin for Phase Framing
- Unfreeze: Communicate why the legacy system cannot continue.
- Change: Train and implement.
- Refreeze: Embed into SOPs and evaluation metrics.

### Use Kotter for Leadership Alignment
- Establish urgency (regulatory risk, reporting inefficiencies).
- Build a cross-functional coalition.
- Show early wins (improved reporting turnaround).

### Use ADKAR for Staff Adoption
- Awareness: Why change is necessary.
- Desire: Address fear and resistance.
- Knowledge: Train.
- Ability: Hands-on practice.
- Reinforcement: Monitor adoption.

---

# Conclusion

A new system implementation in a social services agency succeeds when:

1. Strategy is defined before vendor selection.
2. Stakeholders are aligned early.
3. Data migration is rigorously planned.
4. Governance is formalized.
5. Change management is structured and evidence-based.

Grounding your proposal in established literature (Kotter, Lewin, ADKAR, McKinsey, Bridges) strengthens credibility and reduces implementation risk.
